# Coding Projects
I'm [nhr](https://github.com/nhr) on GitHub. Here are some of my more interesting projects:

### [Shiftzilla](https://github.com/nhr/shiftzilla)
While I was part of the [OpenShift](https://openshift.com/) team, I was responsible for managing several consecutive product releases. Understanding our progress against bug counts was critical to staying on top of our quarterly release cadence. I wrote Shiftzilla to start capturing Bugzilla-based info and offering us a way to analyze long-time trends on a per-release and per-development-team basis.

### [AsciiBinder](https://asciibinder.net)
Another problem we had to solve for OpenShift was managing [documentation](https://docs.openshift.com) for several different versions of the product at once. The fantastic [AsciiDoctor](https://asciidoctor.org) tool could handle converting markdown pages (specifically, AsciiDoc pages) to HTML, but there was no utility at the time that could compile a bunch of AsciiDoctor-generated pages into a single website. I created AsciiBinder to solve that problem.

### [The SWN Sector Generator](https://swn.emichron.com/)
Totally not work-related, but very relevant to one of my hobbies. I took the open-sourced rules for a role playing game called [Stars Without Number](http://www.sinenomine-pub.com/?page_id=395) and created this galaxy generator that has been running almost continuously since 2010.

# Non-Coding Projects

### Quarantine Dance Mix
My DJ persona is [Father Cashmere](https://www.fathercashmere.com/) (my kids came up with it). Starting in April of 2020 I started live streaming a DJ set every Friday night. This was the birth of a listening party / Zoom hangout that has come to be known as the Quarantine Dance Mix. We all thought the pandemic would be behind us after a few months, and I thought the same thing about my DJ sets. But COVID-19 is still here and so I'm still doing these sets on Friday nights. You can learn more about that at the Father Cashmere website.

### [Spartan](https://spartan.com) Obstacle Course Racing
Since 2010 I have participated in at least one obstacle course per year. In 2019 I completed my first Spartan Trifecta, and was scheduled to give it another shot in 2020. Here's hoping 2021 will work out a little better! Training for events like this has been incredibly helpful for me to keep myself sane during a very crazy year.
